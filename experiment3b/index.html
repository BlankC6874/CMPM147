Experiment 3b - Alternate Worlds - Overworld

seed 0

Description

This overworld generator paints coherent grass and water biomes using Perlin noise, then sparsely sprinkles trees. Half of the grass and water tiles animate in a gentle loop to simulate wind and ripples, while the others remain static for a natural, non‑uniform look. Drifting cloud shadows add extra atmosphere. Real‑time ASCII editing and reseed provide instant feedback.

Technical

- Grass (`.`) uses three frames at tileset coords (0,0), (1,0), (2,0) with selective animation.
- Water (`~`) uses three frames at coords (0,13), (1,13), (2,13) with Perlin‑driven variation.
- Trees (`T`) are chosen from tileset region ti=20…27, tj=3…7 and placed at random on grass.
- Autotiling: `gridCheck` + `gridCode` encode 4‑way neighbor presence into 0–15, `drawContext` selects edge vs. center via a lookup table.
- Animation: `(i+j)&1` divides tiles into static vs. animated groups; Perlin noise and `millis()` control frame selection and cloud shadow overlays.

Reflection

Implementing selective animation taught me how to balance motion with stillness for a believable landscape. Fine‑tuning Perlin thresholds and frame arrays eliminated jarring tiles and produced smooth transitions. Finding the correct tree tile region required image sampling and experimentation. Overall, this experiment demonstrates how simple procedural rules can yield rich, dynamic overworlds that feel alive.
